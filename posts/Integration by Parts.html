#math #integration #calculus

Motivation · When there is a complex sum being integrated we can sometimes use IBP (integration by Parts) to simplify the integrand and may make solving the resulting integral easier.

formula · \[ \int udv = uv - \int v du \] To decide which multiplicand is \(u\) or \(v\) the consideration should be with which would be easier to integrate and differentiate thus helping reduce the complexity of the calculation.

Example of integral where integration by parts makes sense to use. \[ \int x^2\ln (x) dx \] [^1]: example · 1

Let \(u = \ln(x)\), \(dv = x^2\) and then \(du = \frac{1}{x}\) and \(v = \frac{1}{3} x^3\) \[ \begin{align} \int x^2\ln (x) dx &= \ln(x) \frac{x^3}{3} - \int \frac{x^3}{3} * \frac{1}{x} dx \\ &= \ln(x) \frac{x^3}{3} - \frac{1}{3} \int x^3 * x^{-1} dx\\ &= \ln(x) \frac{x^3}{3} - \frac{1}{3} \int x^2 dx\\ &= \ln(x) \frac{x^3}{3} - \frac{1}{3} \frac{x^3}{3} + C \\ &= \ln(x) \frac{x^3}{3} - \frac{x^3}{9} + C \\ \end{align} \] [^2]: example · 1 · solution

#calculus #integration #math #trig

Motivation · Some integrals can be simplified by substituting trig functions into the integrand.

Method · Substitute the following values depending on the structure of the integrand.

1 · \(a^2 + x^2\), then substitute \(\tan x\) 2 · \(a^2 - x^2\), then substitute \(\sin x\) 3 · \(x^2 - a^2\), then substitute \(\sec x\)

Use [[Calculus Appendix|trig identities]] to simplify the integrand and solve the integral. Then, substitute the original value back into the function.

After the initial substitution the entire function should be in terms of \(\theta\) rather that \(x\). But, the final result should be back in terms of \(x\).

\[ \int \frac{\sqrt{ 9 - x^2 }}{x^2} dx \] \(^1\) Practice problem from exam 1 review Let \(x = 3\sin \theta\), thus \(dx = 3\cos \theta d\theta\)

\[ \begin{align} \int \frac{\sqrt{ 9 - x^2 }}{x^2} dx &= \int \frac{\sqrt{ 9 - 3^2\sin^2\theta}}{(3\sin\theta)^2} 3\cos \theta d\theta\\ &= \int\frac{\sqrt{ 9(1-\sin^2\theta) }}{9\sin^2\theta} 3\cos \theta d\theta\\ &= \int\frac{\sqrt{ 9(\cos^2\theta) }}{9\sin^2\theta} 3\cos \theta d\theta\\ &= \int\frac{ 9\cos^2\theta}{9\sin^2\theta} d\theta\\ &= \int \cot^2\theta d\theta \\ &= \int \csc^2x - 1 d\theta \\ &= -\cot \theta - \theta + C \\ \end{align} \] To return the theta value to x, solve \(x = 3\sin \theta\) for \(\theta\). \[ \arcsin(x/3) = \theta\] And, to replace the \(-\cot \theta\) the easiest way is to use reference angles. \(\cot \theta\) is adjacent over opposite. Since we know \(\sin \theta = \frac{x}{3}\) we know the opposite and hypotenuse, and therefore can derive \(\sqrt{ 9 - x^2 }\) as the adjacent side via Pythagorean’s theorem.

\[ -\cot \theta = - \frac{\sqrt{ 9 - x^2 }}{x} \] Therefore, \[ \begin{align} \int \frac{\sqrt{ 9 - x^2 }}{x^2} dx &= -\cot\theta-\theta + C \\ &= - \frac{\sqrt{ 9 - x^2 }}{x} - \arcsin(\frac{x}{3})+ C ) \end{align} _\square \]
